barricade [,bæri'keid; 'bæri-]

  • n.
    • 街垒，路障
    • 隔板，挡墙，防栅，栅栏
    • 障碍(物)，阻碍
    • [有时用复数] 争论题目；战斗领域
  • vt.
    • 用栅栏阻挡；用挡墙围住；设栅防守
    • 设街垒(或路障)于，用街垒阻塞；阻塞，堵塞，挡住：

      • The soldiers barricaded the streets to prevent an attack.

        士兵们在大街上设置路障，阻止进攻。

      • The police barricaded the streets.

        警察在街上设路障。

    • 使躲在挡墙(或路障等)后头：

      • The armed rebels had barricaded themselves in the old town.

        武装叛乱分子已躲入旧城。

    • 变形：
    • barricaded , barricading
以上来源于：《21世纪大英汉词典》

barricade [ ,bæri'keid, 'bæri- ]

  • n.
    • a barrier set up by police to stop traffic on a street or road in order to catch a fugitive or inspect traffic etc.

      同义词：roadblock

    • a barrier (usually thrown up hastily so as to impede the advance of an enemy)

      "they stormed the barricade"

  • v.
    • render unsuitable for passage

      "barricade the streets"

      同义词：block | blockade | stop | block off | block up | bar

    • prevent access to by barricading

      "The street where the President lives is always barricaded"

    • block off with barricades

      同义词：barricado
