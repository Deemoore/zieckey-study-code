defile1 [di'fail]

  • vt.
    • 使污秽，弄脏，污损，污染；使失去光泽：

      • The animals defiled the water.

        牲口把水弄脏了。

      • be defiled with ink stains

        被墨水渍弄脏

    • 亵渎，使不纯洁：

      • The infidels defiled the holy shrine.

        异教徒亵渎神圣的神殿。

      • to defile the church by using it as a stable

        用教堂做马厩而亵渎教堂

    • 使不贞洁；使败坏，使腐败，腐蚀：

      • Evil books defile the mind.

        坏书会腐蚀(人们的)思想。

    • 玷污，损坏，破坏，糟蹋，败坏(名誉等)：

      • Insults alone cannot defile a person's honour.

        仅靠辱骂不可能败坏一个人的名誉。

    • [古语]奸污(处女)，使(女子)失贞操，破坏(女子)的贞洁
  • 近义词: contaminate
    • 变形：
    • defiled , defiling

defile2 [di'fail; 'di:-]

  • vi.
    • 以单列行进，以纵队前进，鱼贯而入：

      • The students defiled through the gate of the school.

        学生们排成单列通过学校大门。

  • n.
    • (尤指山间的)狭道，隘道，狭径，小路，峡道，溪谷，峡谷；关隘
    • 纵列行进
    • 变形：
    • defiled , defiling
以上来源于：《21世纪大英汉词典》

defile [ di'fail ]

  • n. a narrow pass (especially one between mountains)

    同义词：gorge

  • v.
    • place under suspicion or cast doubt upon

      同义词：sully | corrupt | taint | cloud

    • make dirty or spotty, as by exposure to air; also used metaphorically

      同义词：tarnish | stain | maculate | sully

    • spot, stain, or pollute

      "The townspeople defiled the river by emptying raw sewage into it"

      同义词：foul | befoul | maculate

以上来源于: WordNet
