discretion [dis'kreʃən]

  • n.
    • (言行)谨慎，慎重，审慎
    • 斟酌处理(或决定、选择)的自由(或权限)
    • 决定(力)；判断(力)；辨别力；明辨；明智
    • 【法律】(法院的)酌处权
    • [D-](对贵族和主教的尊称)阁下，大人(与Your， His连用)
    • 【证券】自行处理；处置权
    • [废语]区分；分离
  • 短语:
    • at discretion
      • 随意，任意；由自己酌量[亦作at one's discretion]
      • 无条件地任凭敌方处置
    • at someone's discretion 或 at the discretion of someone 任凭某人自行处理，由某人斟酌决定，随某人的意见
    • Discretion in speech is more than eloquence. [谚语]慎言胜于善辩。
    • Discretion is the better part of valour. [废语]谨慎(或小心)即大勇。
    • fling(或cast,throw)discretion (或caution)to the winds 丢在一边，不顾一切；大胆(或冒险)地行事
    • in(或within)someone's discretion(to do) 任某人自行决定，是某人的自由
    • leave to someone's discretion 由某人自由处理，由某人自行决定
    • surrender at discretion 无条件投降
    • use one's (own) discretion 自己斟酌决定
    • with discretion 慎重地，审慎地
    • years(或the age) of discretion 【法律】解事责任年龄(英、美法律规定为满14岁)
以上来源于：《21世纪大英汉词典》

discretion [ dis'kreʃən ]

  • n.
    • freedom to act or judge on one's own
    • knowing how to avoid embarrassment or distress

      "the servants showed great tact and discretion"

      同义词：discreetness | circumspection | prudence

    • refined taste; tact

      同义词：delicacy

    • the power of making free choices unconstrained by external agencies

      同义词：free will

    • the trait of judging wisely and objectively

      同义词：discernment
