induct [in'dʌkt]

  • vt.
    • (通过仪式)使就职，使正式就任(常与to，into，as连用)：

      • He was formally inducted into the office of governor.

        他正式就任州长之职了。

      • to be inducted to an office of mayor

        正式就任市长

    • 使正式入会；使正式参加(社团组织)(常与to，into连用)：

      • He was inducted into a literary society.

        他被正式接纳为文学学会会员。

      • to be inducted into a new profession

        (某人)加入一个新行业

    • [美国英语]征召…入伍(常与into连用)：

      • to be inducted into the army

        被召入伍

    • 向…介绍(特殊知识、经验、秘诀等)，使入门；使…有所了解(常与to，into连用)：

      • His father inducted him into the secrets of the trade.

        他父亲教给他做生意的秘诀。

    • 引导，带领(常与into连用)：

      • to induct somebody into a seat

        引导某人就座

    • 【电学】感应，感生
    • 【逻辑学】归纳出
以上来源于：《21世纪大英汉词典》

induct [ in'dʌkt ]

  • v.
    • place ceremoniously or formally in an office or position

      "there was a ceremony to induct the president of the Academy"

      同义词：invest | seat

    • accept young people into society, usually with some rite

      同义词：initiate

    • admit as a member

      "We were inducted into the honor society"

    • produce electric current by electrostatic or magnetic processes

      同义词：induce

    • introduce or initiate

      "The young geisha was inducted into the ways of her profession"
