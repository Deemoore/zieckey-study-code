raid [reid]

  • n.
    • 突然袭击
    • (突如其来的)行凶；抢劫
    • (警方采取的)搜捕；突然搜查
    • [美国英语](在商业上对竞争对手采取的)挖取对方人才
    • (为使股票价格猛烈下跌的)集体抛售
    • (公款等的)侵吞(或盗用)
  • vt.
    • 突然袭击：

      • They were going to raid Warsaw the next day.

        他们将于次日袭击华沙。

    • 突然搜查：

      • The police raided their gambling house.

        警察查抄了他们的赌窟。

    • 劫掠；行凶：

      • The bank next to our school was raided last week.

        我们学校附近的那个银行上周被人抢劫了。

      • Many children raided our orchard.

        许多孩子到我们果园里偷水果。

    • 侵吞
    • 挖取(人才等)
  • vi.
    • 发动突然袭击(常与 on或into连用)：

      • Many robbers raided on the jeweller's in the shopping centre.

        许多强盗抢劫了购物中心里的珠宝店。

  • 短语:
    • air raid 空袭
    • raid the market [口语](散布谣言、制造恐慌气氛)搅乱市面以便卖出股票等；扰乱市场
以上来源于：《21世纪大英汉词典》

raid [ reid ]

  • n.
    • a sudden short attack

      同义词：foray | maraud

    • an attempt by speculators to defraud investors
  • v.
    • search without warning, make a sudden surprise attack on

      "The police raided the crack house"

      同义词：bust

    • enter someone else's territory and take spoils

      "The pirates raided the coastal villages regularly"

      同义词：foray into

    • take over (a company) by buying a controlling interest of its stock

      "T. Boone Pickens raided many large companies"

    • search for something needed or desired

      "Our babysitter raided our refrigerator"
