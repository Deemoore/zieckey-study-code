pinch1 [pintʃ]

  • vt.
    • 捏；拧；挤；夹痛：

      • He pinched his fingers in the door.

        他的手指被门夹住了。

    • 掐掉(嫩枝等)；修剪：

      • The gardener pinched out the weak shoot on a plant.

        园林工人掐掉植物上发育不良的芽。

    • 使(植物)枯萎，使凋谢：

      • A heavy frost had pinched the flowers.

        一场严霜使花朵全都凋零了。

    • 使不舒服；使困苦：

      • He is pinched with poverty.

        他为贫穷所苦。

    • 使悲痛；使不安：

      • The lack of a license was pinching me.

        没有执照我感到很不安

    • 使收缩；使消瘦：

      • Her face was pinched with grief.

        她的面容因悲伤而憔悴。

    • 挤压，挤迫：

      • The boy complained the shoe pinched his taes.

        小男孩抱怨鞋太紧挤他的脚趾。

    • 使紧缩；节制；使限制；节约；吝惜：

      • They were ready to pinch themselves for years.

        他们准备使自己过几年拮据的生活。

    • 摄取…放入；少量加入：

      • The cook pinches salt into the soup.

        厨师在汤里放了一些盐。

    • 勒索；榨取：

      • The scoundrel pinched money from the old man.

        恶棍勒索老人的钱财。

    • 【海洋学】使(帆船)紧抢风
    • [英国英语](赛马时)催马快跑
    • (用撬棒)一点一点地撬动(重物)
    • [俚语]盗窃，窃取：

      • Somebody pinched my sweater.

        有人偷了我的运动衫。

    • [俚语]逮捕；拘留；搜捕：

      • He was pinched for speeding.

        他因超速而被拘留。

    • [俚语]用差不多直竖的球杆向下对(弹子)球直戳
  • vi.
    • 挤压；收缩；紧：

      • This shoe pinches.

        这只鞋夹脚。

    • 引起不舒服；引起苦恼：

      • His stomach pinched with hunger.

        他肚子饥饿难受。

    • [美国英语](矿脉、煤层等)狭窄；变薄；尖灭(out)：

      • The road pinched down to a trail.

        大路渐渐变窄，成了羊肠小道。

  • n.
    • 捏；拧；挤；夹
    • 一撮；微量
    • 重压；紧迫
    • 困苦；困难；剧痛
    • 危机，危急；紧急情况，紧要关头
    • 撬杆
    • 匮乏
    • 【物理学】箍缩等离子体
    • [美国英语](矿脉、煤层等的)狭窄点
    • [俚语]逮捕；拘留；指控，控告
    • [俚语]盗窃；剽窃；被窃物
    • [澳大利亚、新西兰方言]陡峭的山
    • [俚语] 极易办到的事，极易得到的东西；毫无疑问的事
  • 近义词: steal
  • 短语:
    • at(或 in,on) a pinch 必要时；在紧要关头；危急时
    • do in a pinch 凑合用一下
    • drop a pinch of salt on the tail of 捕获(或捉住)某人
    • feel the pinch [口语]感到经济困难(或手头拮据)
    • if it comes to the pinch 在万不得已时，在必要时
    • pinch and scrape (或 screw, save) 省吃俭用
    • pinch pennies 精打细算；非常节省
    • when it comes to the pinch ＝ at a pinch
    • with a pinch of salt 不全信地；有保留地，半信半疑地

pinch [ pintʃ ]

  • n.
    • a painful or straitened circumstance

      "the pinch of the recession"

    • an injury resulting from getting some body part squeezed
    • a slight but appreciable addition

      同义词：touch | hint | tinge | mite | jot | speck | soupcon

    • a sudden unforeseen crisis (usually involving danger) that requires immediate action

      同义词：emergency | exigency

    • a small sharp bite or snip

      同义词：nip

    • a squeeze with the fingers

      同义词：tweak

    • the act of apprehending (especially apprehending a criminal)

      同义词：apprehension | arrest | catch | collar | taking into custody

  • v.
    • squeeze tightly between the fingers

      "He pinched her behind"

      同义词：squeeze | twinge | tweet | nip | twitch

    • make ridges into by pinching together

      同义词：crimp

    • make off with belongings of others

      同义词：pilfer | cabbage | purloin | abstract | snarf | swipe | hook | sneak | filch | nobble | lift

    • cut the top off

      同义词：top

    • irritate as if by a nip, pinch, or tear

      "the pain is as if sharp points pinch your back"

      同义词：vellicate
