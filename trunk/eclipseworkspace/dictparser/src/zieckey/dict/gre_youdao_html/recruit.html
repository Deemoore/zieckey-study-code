recruit [ri'kru:t]

  • vt.
    • 招募；征募(新兵)；招收：

      • The wartime cabinet first recruited men into the forces from non-essential industries.

        战时内阁首先从非重点工业中招募人员补充武装力量。

    • (增加人员)充实(部队等)；补充：

      • Before sailing, we recruited our provisions.

        启航前，我们补充了给养。

    • 雇用；聘请：

      • Many young peasants were recruited into the coal mining industry.

        煤炭工业中雇用了很多年轻的农民。

      • They recruited a number of old teachers for the new school.

        他们给这所新学校聘请了一些老教师。

    • 吸收(新成员)：

      • Our football fan club began to recruit new members last week.

        我们的足球球迷俱乐部上周开始招收新会员。

    • [古语]恢复(健康、体力等)：

      • He has recruited his health.

        他现已恢复健康。

  • vi.
    • 吸收新成员(尤指招兵)：

      • They didn't admit they were defeated; they began to recruit and attempted to stage a comeback.

        他们不承认失败，他们开始招兵妄图东山再起。

    • [罕用语]得到充实；(健康、体力等)复原；休养：

      • She is to go to the seaside to recruit.

        她将去海滨休养。

  • n.
    • 新兵
    • 新成员；新手
    • [古语]补给品；供应品
    • 大学新生
    • 招聘
    • 人才开发
    • 就业
    • (日本)利库路特人才开发公司
  • 短语:
    • raw recruit
      • 【军事】新兵
      • 新手，生手
以上来源于：《21世纪大英汉词典》

recruit [ ri'kru:t ]

  • n.
    • a recently enlisted soldier

      同义词：military recruit

    • any new member or supporter (as in the armed forces)

      同义词：enlistee

  • v.
    • register formally as a participant or member

      "The party recruited many new members"

      同义词：enroll | inscribe | enter | enrol

    • seek to employ

      "The lab director recruited an able crew of assistants"

    • cause to assemble or enlist in the military

      "recruit new soldiers"

      同义词：levy | raise
