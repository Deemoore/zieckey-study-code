appropriate [ə'prəuprieit; ə'prəupriət]

  • vt.
    • 把…据为己有，私占，占用，侵吞；专用，独用：

      • That selfish student always appropriates the biggest bookcase and desk.

        那个自私的学生总是占用最大的书橱和书桌。

    • (不适当地)擅用，挪用，盗用：

      • He appropriated the trust funds for himself.

        他挪用信托资金。

    • 拨出(款项等)，拨出(款项等)为专用：

      • The legislature appropriated funds for the university.

        立法机关拨款作为大学经费。

    • 顺手牵羊，不告而取，偷窃(尤指零钱、小件物品等)
  • adj.
    • 合适的，适合的，适宜的，适当的，恰当的，恰如其分的，相称的(for，to)
    • 政治上正确的[亦作PC(politically correct)]
    • [罕用语]专属的；自己的；特定的
  • 近义词: fit
    • 变形：
    • appropriated , appropriating
以上来源于：《21世纪大英汉词典》

appropriate [ ə'prəuprieit, ə'prəupriət ]

  • v.
    • give or assign a resource to a particular person or cause

      同义词：allow | earmark | set aside | reserve

    • take possession of by force, as after an invasion

      同义词：capture | seize | conquer

  • adj.
    • suitable for a particular person or place or condition etc

      "a book not appropriate for children"; " a funeral conducted the appropriate solemnity"; " it seems that an apology is appropriate"

    • appropriate for achieving a particular end; implies a lack of concern for fairness

      同义词：advantageous

    • meant or adapted for an occasion or use

      "not an appropriate (or fit) time for flippancy"

      同义词：suitable | suited

    • suitable and fitting

      同义词：harmonious

    • being of striking appropriateness and pertinence

      同义词：apposite | apt | pertinent

以上来源于: WordNet
