frisk [frisk]

  • vt.
    • 使蹦跳，嬉戏，雀跃；轻快地摇动：

      • The puppy frisked its tail.

        小狗轻摇尾巴。

    • [俚语]迅速搜查；尤指搜(某人)的身体(有无武器或赃物等)：

      • The passengers were frisked before they were allowed to board the plane.

        乘客们在被允许登上飞机前先被搜身。

    • [俚语]扒窃：

      • The pickpocket frisked him of his wallet.

        扒手把他的钱包偷走了。

  • vi.
    • 欢跃；雀跃，蹦蹦跳跳；嬉戏：

      • lambs frisking in the meadow

        在草地上跳来跳去的小羊

  • n.
    • 跳跃，雀跃；嬉戏；欢乐；欢欣鼓舞
    • [俚语]欢乐者的动作，跳跳蹦蹦
    • [俚语]搜查，搜身
  • adj.
    • [古语]活跃的，活泼的；充满生气的
以上来源于：《21世纪大英汉词典》

frisk [ frisk ]

  • n. the act of searching someone for concealed weapons or illegal drugs

    "he gave the suspect a quick frisk"

    同义词：frisking

  • v.
    • play boisterously

      同义词：frolic | lark | rollick | skylark | disport | sport | cavort | gambol | romp | run around | lark about

    • search as for concealed weapons by running the hands rapidly over the clothing and through the pockets

      "The police frisked everyone at the airport"
