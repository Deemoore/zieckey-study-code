hot [hɔt]

  • adj.
    • 热的
    • (令人)感到热的；烫的，灼热的，炎热的
    • (使身体)发热的，体热的
    • 刺激性的；辣的，辛辣的
    • (颜色)给人热感的；暖色的
    • 热烈的；热切的；狂热的
    • 热情的，热心的
    • 激动的，急躁的，发火的
    • 激烈的，猛烈的
    • (野兽的气味等)强烈的；(踪迹)新鲜的
    • 时新的，最新的；最近的
    • (新闻报道)刚发表的；(传播媒介)快捷的
    • (食品)刚做好的，才出锅的，才出炉的
    • 刚出版的，才发行的
    • (儿童游戏)接近目标物的；差一点(就猜中)的
    • 流行的，热门的；紧缺的，销路好的
    • [口语]时新的，时髦的；极风行的，轰动一时的
    • [美国俚语]极走运的，有手气的
    • [俚语]紧跟的；接近的
    • [俚语]滑稽的；荒诞的，不可信的
    • [俚语]骇人听闻的；臭名昭著的
    • [俚语]
      • 刚偷来的；非法得来的
      • 危险的
    • 通高压电的
    • 放射性强的
    • [口语]极可能成功(或获胜)的；竞技状态极佳的；(运动员)技术高超的
    • (球赛中的球)重击的，飞快的，令对手难应接的
    • (爵士音乐)节奏快的；(音乐)激动人心的
    • (金属加工过程中)加热的
    • 内容详细的，资料翔实的，细节详尽的；信息丰富而无需观众(或听众、读者)参与的
    • (电话等)直通的，“热线”的
    • [俚语](车辆、飞机等)(可以)超高速驾驶的；(飞机)着陆速度快的
    • 好色的，淫荡的；欲火中烧的
    • [俚语]极好的，呱呱叫的；令人满意的
    • [美国俚语]身体棒的；体型好的
    • [美国俚语]有生气的，活跃的，繁忙的
    • [美国俚语]疯狂撒野的
    • [美国俚语](子弹、火箭等)正在发射的
  • adv.
    • 热；灼热
    • 热切地，热望地
    • 生气地，愤怒地
    • 热烈地；猛烈地
    • (金属加工)加热地
  • vt.
    • 加热，温热(食品)(常与 up连用)：

        稍等一等，我很快就能把你的奶热好。

    • 使激动，使活跃；使烦躁(与up连用)：

        会谈的气氛变得活跃了。

      • He was hotted up by her constant chatter.

        她的喋喋不休令他烦躁不安。

    • 变热(与up连用)
    • 变得激动起来；变得活跃起来；变得危险起来；加剧；加快(与up连用)：

      • The competition between the two companies is hotting up at last.

        两个公司间的竞争最终变得越来越厉害。

hot [ hɔt ]

  • adj.
    • used of physical heat; having a high or higher than desirable temperature or giving off heat or feeling or causing a sensation of heat or burning

      "hot stove"; " hot water"; " a hot August day"; " a hot stuffy room"; " she's hot and tired"; " a hot forehead"

    • characterized by violent and forceful activity or movement; very intense

      "the fighting became hot and heavy"; " a hot engagement"

      同义词：raging

    • extended meanings; especially of psychological heat; marked by intensity or vehemence especially of passion or enthusiasm

      "a hot temper"; " a hot topic"; " a hot new book"; " a hot love affair"; " a hot argument"

    • (color) bold and intense

      "hot pink"

    • sexually excited or exciting

      "was hot for her"; " hot pants"

    • recently stolen or smuggled

      "hot merchandise"; " a hot car"

    • very fast; capable of quick response and great speed

      "a hot sports car"; " got off to a hot start"; " in hot pursuit"; " a red-hot line drive"

      同义词：blistering | red-hot

    • wanted by the police

      "a hot suspect"

    • being very spicy

      "hot salsa"; " jalapeno peppers are very hot"

    • performed or performing with unusually great skill and daring and energy

      "a hot drummer"; " he's hot tonight"

    • very popular or successful

      "one of the hot young talents"; " cabbage patch dolls were hot last season"

    • very unpleasant or even dangerous

      "make it hot for him"; " in the hot seat"; " in hot water"

    • newest or most recent

      "news hot off the press"; " red-hot information"

      同义词：red-hot

    • having or bringing unusually good luck

      "hot at craps"; " the dice are hot tonight"

    • very good; often used in the negative

      "he's hot at math but not so hot at history"

    • newly made

      "a hot scent"

    • having or showing great eagerness or enthusiasm

      "hot for travel"

    • of a seeker; very near to the object sought

      "you are hot"

    • having or dealing with dangerously high levels of radioactivity

      "hot fuel rods"; " a hot laboratory"

    • charged or energized with electricity

      "a hot wire"

      同义词：live

    • marked by excited activity

      "a hot week on the stock market"
