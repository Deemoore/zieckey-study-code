conceive [kən'si:v]

  • vt.
    • 想到；构想出：

      • He conceived a new plan very quickly.

        他很快就想出一个新的计划方案。

    • 想像，形成…概念：

      • I can't conceive why you allowed your daughter to travel alone.

        我想不通你为什么会让你的女儿独自外出旅游。

    • 以为，认为，想：

      • In ancient times the world was conceived of as flat.

        在古代地球被认为是扁平的。

    • 怀有(某种感情)：

      • to conceive a prejudice against someone

        对某人抱有成见

    • (用言语)表达：

      • His speech was conceived in simple language.

        他的讲演是用通俗易懂的措辞表达出来的。

    • 怀(孕)：

      • Their first child was conceived in January and born in October.

        他们的第一个孩子是在一月怀上、十月出生的。

    • [常用于被动语态]开始；发起，引起；产生；创立，创建：

      • a new nation conceived in liberty

        一个以自由为立国之本的新国家

    • [古语]理解；了解
  • vi.
    • 想到，想出；设想(常与of连用)：

      • We can't conceive of living without a telephone.

        我们简直设想不出没有电话人怎么活下去。

    • 怀孕：

      • The doctor told her that she couldn't conceive again.

        医生告诉她不能再怀孕了。

    • 变形：
    • conceived , conceiving
以上来源于：《21世纪大英汉词典》

conceive [ kən'si:v ]

  • v.
    • have the idea for

      "He conceived of a robot that would help paralyzed patients"; " This library was well conceived"

      同义词：gestate | conceptualize | conceptualise

    • judge or regard; look upon; judge

      "The racist conceives such people to be inferior"

      同义词：think | believe | consider

    • become pregnant; undergo conception

      "She cannot conceive"; " My daughter was conceived in Christmas Day"
