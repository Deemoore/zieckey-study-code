plead [pli:d]

  • vi.
    • 辩护；辩论：

      • The woman charged with murder was said to be mad and unfit to plead.

        被控犯谋杀罪的女人据说疯了，因此不适合出庭申辩。

    • 抗辩；答辩：

      • to plead in court

        在法庭上作答辩

    • 恳求，请求：

      • He pleaded with us to let him go to the rock concert.

        他请求我们让他去听摇滚音乐会。

  • vt.
    • 为(案件)辩护：

      • His mother did her best to plead his case.

        他母亲尽力为他的案子辩护。

    • 以…作为答辩；抗辩：

      • She pleaded that she was unaware that these were stolen goods.

        她答辩说她不知道这些东西是偷来的。

    • 向法庭陈述(案情)：

      • They employed the best lawyer they could get to plead their case.

        他们聘请了能请到的最好的律师为他们陈述案情。

    • 承认；认(罪)：

      • They made her plead madness.

        他们要她承认自己精神错乱。

    • 以…为理由，以…为借口：

      • I'm sorry I didn't answer your letter.I can only plead forgetfulness.

        对不起，我没有回信，我只能归咎于自己的健忘。

    • 请求，恳求：

      • Her parents pleaded that she should be given one more chance.

        她父母请求再给她一次机会。

  • 近义词: appeal
  • 短语:
    • plead guilty 见guilty
    • plead the Fifth (Amendment) 【法律】因怕证实自己有罪拒不申辩；拒不解释
    • plead with someone (for) (为某事)恳求某人；向某人恳求
    • 变形：
    • pleaded , pled或 plead , pleading
以上来源于：《21世纪大英汉词典》

plead [ pli:d ]

  • v.
    • appeal or request earnestly

      "I pleaded with him to stop"

    • offer as an excuse or plea

      "She was pleading insanity"

    • enter a plea, as in courts of law

      "She pleaded not guilty"

    • make an allegation in an action or other legal proceeding, especially answer the previous pleading of the other party by denying facts therein stated or by alleging new facts
