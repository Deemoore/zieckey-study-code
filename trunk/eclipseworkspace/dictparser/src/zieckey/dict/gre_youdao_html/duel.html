duel ['dju:əl]

  • n.
    • 决斗
    • (两人或两党之间的)竞争，抗争，争斗，斗争，冲突
  • vi.
    • 决斗：

      • They had been dueling for hours and finally called a draw.

        他们一直决斗了数小时，最后打成平局。

    • 斗争，争斗，冲突：

      • The two parties duelled for power.

        两党为权力而斗争。

  • vt.
    • 与…决斗：

      • Jack duelled his opponent for hours.

        杰克与他对手决斗了数小时。

    • [废语]在决斗中杀死(或战胜)
    • 变形：
    • duelled或-eled , duelling或dueling
以上来源于：《21世纪大英汉词典》

duel [ 'dju:əl ]

  • n.
    • a prearranged fight with deadly weapons by two people (accompanied by seconds) in order to settle a quarrel over a point of honor

      同义词：affaire d'honneur

    • any struggle between two skillful opponents (individuals or groups)
  • v. fight a duel, as over one's honor or a woman

    "In the 19th century, men often dueled over small matters"

以上来源于: WordNet
