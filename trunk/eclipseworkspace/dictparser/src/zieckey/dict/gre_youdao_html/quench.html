quench [kwentʃ]

  • vt.
    • 扑灭，熄灭：

      • He quenched the fire with water.

        他用水将火扑灭。

      • The blaze was quenched by the brave firemen.

        大火被勇敢的消防队员扑灭了。

    • 压倒；压制：

      • The government succeeded in quenching the riot.

        政府成功平息了暴乱。

    • 满足；使缓和：

      • She quenched her thirst with a glass of Coke.

        她喝了一杯可乐解渴。

    • 【冶金学】淬火；使骤冷
  • vi.
    • 熄灭；停止；平静：

      • The large fire quenched at last.

        大火终于熄灭了。

以上来源于：《21世纪大英汉词典》

quench [ kwentʃ ]

  • v.
    • satisfy (thirst)

      "The cold water quenched his thirst"

      同义词：slake | allay | assuage

    • put out, as of fires, flames, or lights

      "quench the flames"

      同义词：snuff out | blow out | extinguish

    • electronics: suppress (sparking) when the current is cut off in an inductive circuit, or suppress (an oscillation or discharge) in a component or device
    • suppress or crush completely

      "quench a rebellion"

      同义词：squelch | quell

    • reduce the degree of (luminescence or phosphorescence) in (excited molecules or a material) by adding a suitable substance
    • cool (hot metal) by plunging into cold water or other liquid

      "quench steel"
