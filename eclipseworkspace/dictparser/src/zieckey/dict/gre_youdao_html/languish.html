languish ['læŋɡwiʃ]

  • vi.
    • 失去活力；变得衰弱无力；枯萎，凋谢：

      • The leaves languished from lack of water.

        树叶因缺水而枯萎。

    • 受折磨；受苦；呻吟：

      • to languish for years in poverty

        常年为贫穷所困

    • 松懈，松弛，懈怠：

      • Our vigilance never languished.

        我们的警惕性从来没有放松过。

    • 焦虑，苦思；渴望：

      • The mother languished for the sight of her daughter.

        母亲苦苦地渴望见到女儿。

    • 显得亲切伤感的样子，做出含情脉脉的伤感之态：

      • The girl languished at him with her staring eyes.

        女孩目不转睛呆呆地望着他。

以上来源于：《21世纪大英汉词典》

languish [ 'læŋɡwiʃ ]

  • v.
    • lose vigor, health, or flesh, as through grief

      同义词：pine away | waste

    • have a desire for something or someone who is not present

      同义词：ache | yearn | yen | pine

    • become feeble

      "The prisoner has be languishing for years in the dungeon"

      同义词：fade

以上来源于: WordNet
