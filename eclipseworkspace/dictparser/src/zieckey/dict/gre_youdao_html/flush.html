flush [flʌʃ]

基本释义 词组短语 同近义词

  • n. 激动，洋溢；面红；萌芽；旺盛；奔流
  • vt. 使齐平；发红，使发亮；用水冲洗；使激动
  • vi. 发红，脸红；奔涌；被冲洗
  • adj. 大量的；齐平的；丰足的，洋溢的；挥霍的

back flush 反冲洗；逆向清洗

flush type 平面型，嵌入式

flush1 [flʌʃ]

  • vi.
    • (因窘迫、愤怒、激动等)(脸)发红；(人)脸红：

      • She began to flush with excitement.

        她由于激动脸开始发红。

      • He flushed when he saw her.

        他一看到她就脸发红。

    • 突然发红，发亮：

      • The aurora flushed into the sky.

        曙光映红天空。

    • (水等)逆流，奔流；涌；泛滥：

      • The blood flushed into his cheeks.

        血液涌向他的面颊。

      • Water flushed through the pipes.

        水从管子奔流而过。

    • (用水等)冲洗干净；冲走：

      • The toilet flushed.

        抽水马桶放水冲洗。

    • 【植物学】发芽
  • vt.
    • 使(人)脸红，使(脸等)发红；使发光；使发热：

      • Anger flushed his cheeks.

        愤怒使他满脸通红。

      • Her forehead was flushed with fever.

        她的前额由于发烧而发烫。

      • The setting sun flushed the mountain tops.

        夕阳映红山顶。

    • 使兴奋，使激动；激励；使振奋；使得意[通常用于被动语态]：

      • The young parents were flushed with happiness at the birth of their first son.

        第一个孩子出生的喜悦使得年轻夫妇兴奋不已。

      • The team was flushed with its first victory.

        这个队因初次胜利而感振奋。

    • (用水)冲洗，冲刷(下水道、厕所等)：

      • The engineer decided to flush the hold of the ship.

        轮机师决定用水冲洗船舱。

    • 排水：

      • to flush water from flood hand

        将受淹土地的水排除

    • 使注满；淹没；漫灌(草地)：

      • The river flushed the meadow.

        河水淹没了草地。

    • 对(家畜)进行催情补饲
    • 结束(一天的工作)
    • 拒绝(某人)；辞退(某人)
    • 【计算机】(表面)划掉
  • n.
    • 脸红，泛红，红光，红晕
    • 潮红；发烧，发热
    • (水)急流，奔流；涌；泛滥；冲洗，冲刷
    • 萌发，旺盛，活力，精力充沛
    • 兴奋，激动，振奋，情绪突然高涨
    • 茁壮成长；嫩芽；嫩草
    • (绝经或发热时的)灼热感
  • 短语:
    • flush it
      • [美国俚语]
      • 失败；不及格
      • [表示不信、轻蔑语]见鬼去吧
    • flush of dawn [诗歌用语]晨曦，曙光
    • flush of hope 希望的曙光；希望的实现
    • in the first flush
      • 一时兴起
      • 处于盛年，青春焕发的时期

flush2 [flʌʃ]

  • adj.
    • (尤指金钱)充足的，充裕的；富裕的，繁荣的
    • 大量的，丰富的
    • 面色红润的，血色好的；发红的
    • 齐平的，同高的，同一平面的
    • 直接的，正面的；正打中的
    • 充满活力的，精力充沛的
    • 注满的，泛滥的
    • 【印刷】顶格，不缩排的
    • 【船舶学】平甲板的
  • adv.
    • 齐平地；同高地；对齐地；成一直线地
    • 直接地；面对面地；恰好地
  • vt.
    • 使成水平，使齐平，把…弄平；嵌平(接合处)：

      • The mason flushed the joint with mortar.

        泥工用灰浆把接缝处抹平。

    • 【狩猎】

flush [ flʌʃ ]

  • n.
    • the period of greatest prosperity or productivity

      同义词：flower | prime | peak | heyday | bloom | blossom | efflorescence

    • a rosy color (especially in the cheeks) taken as a sign of good health

      同义词：bloom | blush | rosiness

    • sudden brief sensation of heat (associated with menopause and some mental disorders)

      同义词：hot flash

    • a poker hand with all 5 cards in the same suit
    • the swift release of a store of affective force

      同义词：bang | boot | charge | rush | thrill | kick

    • a sudden rapid flow (as of water)

      "he heard the flush of a toilet"

      同义词：gush | outpouring

    • sudden reddening of the face (as from embarrassment or guilt or shame or modesty)

      同义词：blush

  • v.
    • turn red, as if in embarrassment or shame

      同义词：blush | crimson | redden

    • flow freely

      "The garbage flushed down the river"

    • make level or straight

      同义词：level | even out | even

    • polish and make shiny

      同义词：buff | burnish | furbish

    • rinse, clean, or empty with a liquid

      "flush the wound with antibiotics"

      同义词：scour | purge

    • irrigate with water from a sluice

      同义词：sluice

    • cause to flow or flood with or as if with water

      "flush the meadows"

  • adj.
    • of a surface exactly even with an adjoining one, forming the same plane

      "a door flush with the wall"; " the bottom of the window is flush with the floor"

    • having an abundant supply of money or possessions of value

      "a speculator flush with cash"

      同义词：affluent | loaded | moneyed | wealthy

  • adv.
    • squarely or solidly

      "hit him flush in the face"

    • in the same plane

      "set it flush with the top of the table"
