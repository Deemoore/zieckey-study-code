prescribed

  • vt. prescribe的变形

prescribe [pris'kraib]

  • vt.
    • 命令；指示；规定：

      • The supervisor prescribed the steps in which orders must be filled out.

        主管规定了完成订货的步骤。

    • 处方；开药；嘱咐(疗法)：

      • The doctor prescribed his patient who was down with fever.

        医生给发烧病人开了药方。

    • 限制，限定：

      • The criminal was prescribed to one poor solitary place.

        罪犯被限制在一个贫穷冷落的地方。

    • 【法律】使(过期限而)失效(或不合法)
  • vi.
    • 命令；指示；规定：

      • to do as the laws prescribe

        依法办事

    • 开药方，给医嘱：

      • to prescribe to a patient for a complaint

        给病人开处方治病

    • 【法律】(通过长期占有等而)要求(权利等)；(因过期限而)失效，不合法：

      • Various rights prescribe in twenty years.

        各种权利时效为20年。

    • 变形：
    • prescribed , prescribing
以上来源于：《21世纪大英汉词典》

prescribed [ pri'skraibd ]

  • adj.
    • set down as a rule or guide
    • fixed or established especially by order or command

      同义词：appointed | decreed | ordained

    • conforming to set usage, procedure, or discipline

      "in prescribed order"

      同义词：official

    • formally laid down or imposed

      同义词：positive
