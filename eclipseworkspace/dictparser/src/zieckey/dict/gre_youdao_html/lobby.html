lobby ['lɔbi]

  • n.
    • (公寓、旅馆、剧院等公共建筑物的)门厅，门廊，前厅，穿堂，休息室
    • 议院走廊；议院休息室；议院接待室
    • [主英国英语]=division lobby
    • [美国英语](在议院走廊通过游说等手段说服议员支持某议案的)院外活动集团，游说集团；压力集团
  • vi.
    • (在议院走廊)对议员进行游说以影响其投票：

      • He lobbied on behalf of businessmen.

        他为商人们游说。

    • 进行游说：

      • They are lobbying for their own proposals.

        他们在为自己的提议进行游说。

  • vt.
    • 通过活动影响(议员)，对(议员)进行游说：

      • John is lobbying some members of the legislature.

        约翰在对一些立法委员们进行游说。

    • 采取游说手段施加影响使(议案等)得以通过(常与through连用)：

      • The ecologists successfully lobbied the bill through.

        生态学家们进行游说成功地使该法案得以通过。

    • 变形：
    • lobbies
    • lobbied , lobbying
以上来源于：《21世纪大英汉词典》

lobby [ 'lɔbi ]

  • n.
    • a large entrance or reception room or area

      同义词：anteroom | antechamber | entrance hall | hall | foyer | vestibule

    • the people who support some common cause or business or principle or sectional interest
    • a group of people who try actively to influence legislation

      同义词：pressure group | third house

  • v. detain in conversation by or as if by holding on to the outer garments of; as for political or economic favors

    同义词：buttonhole
