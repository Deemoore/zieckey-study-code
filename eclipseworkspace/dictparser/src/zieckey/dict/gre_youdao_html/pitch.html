    • 用沥青涂；用沥青覆盖：

      • pitched paper

  • vt.
    • 搭(帐)；扎(营)：

      • They pitched camp by the river.

        他们在河边扎营。

    • 投；掷；扔：

      • Some people just pitch their rubbish over the wall.

        有人径直把垃圾扔过墙去。

    • 投掷(硬币、套环)游戏：

      • boys pitching pennies

        扔硬币的男孩子们

    • 排列；定顺序；把…定在；把…定得：

      • The program was pitched at just the right level.

        大纲所定水平恰到好处。

    • 【音乐】为…定调，为…定音高：

      • The song is pitched too high for my voice.

        对我的嗓子来说，这首歌的音定得太高了。

    • 把…定于特定角度；使倾斜：

      • The carpenters pitched the roof steeply.

        木匠把屋顶造得过于陡斜。

    • 把…定于特定程度(或价值、性质、标准等)：

      • The general's speech was pitched as a call to arms.

        将军的讲话是一个要大家拿起武器的号召。

    • 使带有特定感情地说(或写)：

      • They pitched a story about finding the jewels.

        他们编造了一个找到宝石的故事。

    • 使摔倒；使丢失：

      • The boat overturned and the passengers were pitched out.

        船翻了，乘客被摔了出来。

        约翰给他掷了一个快球，而他把球打出去了。

    • 陈列(推销货物)以供出售，展销：

      • pitching a new line of refrigerators

        大力推销一种新型电冰箱

    • 【高尔夫球】打高球(尤指接近时)；【板球】使(投给击球员的球)定点击地：

      • to pitch the ball up a bit

        把球投高了一点

    • [古语]把…列阵

pitch [ pitʃ ]

  • n.
    • the property of sound that varies with variation in the frequency of vibration
    • (baseball) the act of throwing a baseball by a pitcher to a batter

      同义词：delivery

    • a vendor's position (especially on the sidewalk)

      "he was employed to see that his paper's news pitches were not trespassed upon by rival vendors"

    • promotion by means of an argument and demonstration

      同义词：sales talk | sales pitch

    • degree of deviation from a horizontal plane

      "the roof had a steep pitch"

      同义词：rake | slant

    • any of various dark heavy viscid substances obtained as a residue

      同义词：tar

    • a high approach shot in golf

      同义词：pitch shot

    • an all-fours game in which the first card led is a trump

      同义词：auction pitch

    • abrupt up-and-down motion (as caused by a ship or other conveyance)

      "the pitching and tossing was quite exciting"

      同义词：lurch | pitching

    • the action or manner of throwing something

      "his pitch fell short and his hat landed on the floor"

  • v.
    • throw or toss with a light motion

      同义词：flip | toss | sky

    • move abruptly

      同义词：lurch | shift

    • fall or plunge forward

      "She pitched over the railing of the balcony"

    • set to a certain pitch

      "He pitched his voice very low"

    • sell or offer for sale from place to place

      同义词：peddle | monger | huckster | hawk | vend

    • be at an angle

      同义词：slope | incline

    • heel over

      同义词：cant | cant over | tilt | slant

    • erect and fasten

      "pitch a tent"

      同义词：set up

    • throw or hurl from the mound to the batter, as in baseball

      "The pitcher delivered the ball"

      同义词：deliver

    • hit (a golf ball) in a high arc with a backspin
    • lead (a card) and establish the trump suit
    • set the level or character of

      "She pitched her speech to the teenagers in the audience"

      同义词：gear
