disguise [dis'ɡaiz]

  • vt.
    • (为隐瞒身份或迷惑别人而)改变外貌(或装束)，把(外表、声音等)伪装起来，把…假扮(或假装、乔装)起来，假扮，化装，把…假扮成：

      • The president was disguised as a peasant.

        总统假扮成农民。

      • The enemy soldier disguised as civilian.

        一名敌兵伪装成了平民。

    • 伪装：

      • to disguise one's voice.

        伪装自己的声音，掩藏自己的嗓音

      • Mary disguised her handwriting by writing with her left hand.

        玛丽用左手写字以伪装自己的笔迹。

    • 掩饰；隐瞒，隐藏，掩盖(…的存在，真相或性质)：

      • She tried to disguise her real feelings.

        她试图掩饰自己的真实情感。

      • to disguise an emotion

        掩饰感情

      • to disguise one's intentions

        隐瞒自己的意图

    • [废语]损坏…的外形；使变形
  • n.
    • 伪装衣，伪装物，掩饰物，用于隐瞒身份、性质或质量等的东西，用来伪装的东西(或行动)；化装服饰(或用具、设备)；欺骗性的外罩(或情况、举止等)
    • 表演者的化装(或面具、服装)
    • 伪装，假扮，化装
    • 假装，佯装，做作，掩饰；伪装的行为；借口，托辞
    • 伪装的方式(或形式、状态)
  • 短语:
    • a blessing in disguise 见blessing
    • in disguise 伪装的；化装的，装扮的
    • in(或under) the disguise of 在…伪装下，伪装成，装扮成，以…为借口，在…的幌子下
    • make no disguise of 不掩饰…
    • throw off one's disguise(或mask) 摘下假面具，露出真面目
    • 变形：
    • disguised , disguising
以上来源于：《21世纪大英汉词典》

disguise [ dis'ɡaiz ]

  • n.
    • an outward semblance that misrepresents the true nature of something

      "the theatrical notion of disguise is always associated with catastrophe in his stories"

      同义词：camouflage

    • any attire that modifies the appearance in order to conceal the wearer's identity
    • the act of concealing the identity of something by modifying its appearance

      "he is a master of disguise"

      同义词：camouflage

  • v. make unrecognizable

    "The herb disguises the garlic taste"; " We disguised our faces before robbing the bank"
