concord [ 'kɔŋkɔ:d, kən'kɔ:d ]

  • n.
    • capital of the state of New Hampshire; located in south central New Hampshire on the Merrimack river

      同义词：capital of New Hampshire

    • a harmonious state of things in general and of their properties (as of colors and sounds); congruity of parts with one another and with the whole

      同义词：harmony | concordance

    • the determination of grammatical inflection on the basis of word relations

      同义词：agreement

    • town in eastern Massachusetts near Boston where the first battle of the American Revolution was fought
    • agreement of opinions

      同义词：harmony | concordance

    • the first battle of the American Revolution (April 19, 1775)

      同义词：Lexington | Lexington and Concord

  • v.
    • go together

      "Their ideas concorded"

      同义词：harmonize | harmonise | consort | accord | fit in | agree

    • arrange by concord or agreement
    • arrange the words of a text so as to create a concordance

      "The team concorded several thousand nouns, verbs, and adjectives"

    • be in accord; be in agreement

      "Both philosophers concord on this point"

      同义词：agree | hold | concur
